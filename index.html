Strategic Insights

2026 readout
Key insight

AI and agentic automation is the battleground in 2026

Almost every rival is either launching a dedicated agent product or using AI as the core of their evaluation story. For Continia, a sharp and credible AI narrative is now an entry requirement.

Market structure

Consolidation is shifting focus toward payment rails

Several rivals are now owned by payments or PE giants, pushing them toward monetising payment volume rather than the BC flow itself.

Platform risk

The sharpest BC-native threat is Microsoft itself

Microsoft's Expense Agent (Apr 2026) shows the platform owner moving into ISV territory — and BC 2026 Wave 1 (Apr 30) adds MCP Server support and custom AL agents, letting businesses build their own automation directly inside BC without a third-party ISV.

E-invoicing

Continia competes inside BC's own connector layer

BC's built-in E-Documents/Peppol support places Continia next to Pagero, B2Brouter and Qvalia. The battleground is expanding fast: France B2B e-invoicing is mandatory from September 2026 (large/mid-size), joining Belgium (Jan 2026) — with Germany, Spain and Norway to follow by 2027.

Positioning

Neutral and BC-native still reads as a strong wedge

Payment-owned competitors make Continia's deep, embedded Business Central position easier to explain as focused rather than generic.

Analyst signal

Forrester: agentic branding is widespread, real autonomy is rare

Forrester's April 2026 AP report warns enterprises to evaluate on production performance — accuracy, exception reduction, matching success — not feature lists or demos. This sets up a credibility test that favours vendors with proven track records over those making agentic claims.
