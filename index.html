Key Insight

AI & agentic automation is the battleground in 2026

Almost every rival is either launching a dedicated agent product (Dooap Studio, Tipalti agents, Medius autonomous AP, Tungsten TotalAgility) or has AI as its very core (Stampli). For Continia, this means a sharp, credible AI narrative is no longer a nice-to-have — it's the entry requirement to be taken seriously in evaluations.

Consolidation is shifting focus toward payment rails

Several rivals are now owned by payments or PE giants (AvidXchange → TPG/Corpay, MineralTree → Global Payments) or are growing through acquisitions (onPhase, SignUp/DynamicWeb). This pushes them toward monetising payment volume rather than the BC flow itself — opening a position for Continia as neutral, 100% BC-native finance automation. In practice only ExFlow and Zetadocs are direct BC-native rivals; the rest compete more broadly across ERPs.

The sharpest BC-native threat: Microsoft itself

The search added four BC-native players — Lasernet (output), AMC Banking (payments) and Acubiz (expense) each target a different Continia product pillar. Most notably, Microsoft introduced an AI-powered Expense Agent native to Business Central in April 2026. When the platform owner builds functionality directly into the platform, it puts pressure on third-party solutions — but it also underscores Continia’s strength: deep, unified “built inside” functionality across AP, banking, output and expense, not just one corner.

Continia also competes in BC's own e-invoicing connectors

Business Central's built-in E-Documents/Peppol support relies on APIs from Avalara, B2Brouter, Continia, Logiq, Pagero and SignUp — placing Continia eDocuments side by side with dedicated network players. With e-invoicing mandates rolling out across the EU (incl. Belgium from Jan 1, 2026), this is a fast-growing battleground. Added in this run: Pagero (Thomson Reuters-owned network), B2Brouter and Qvalia.

YouTube Insight

Medius dominates YouTube — with fewer subscribers than Continia

Medius pulls 147,000 views per month with only 326 subscribers — 45× Continia's monthly views, yet with 3× fewer followers. This suggests they are hitting YouTube's search and recommendation algorithm. Stampli shows the same pattern: 57K views/month from just 48 videos. Continia's negative monthly view figure (−7.3K) is most likely YouTube's ongoing cleanup of invalid views rather than real regression — but it is worth investing in content that can capture the algorithm's favour the way Medius and Stampli do. Dooap has gone quiet on their channel over the last 30 days despite aggressive AI activity elsewhere.

Methodology & Caveats

  • What's included: product releases, press, M&A and positioning from publicly available sources. Sources are clickable inside each card.
  • Snapshot: data is from this run — not live. The report is regenerated each time the competitor list is run through (e.g. monthly).
  • New BC-native players: Lasernet (output), AMC Banking (payments), Acubiz (expense) and Microsoft's own Expense Agent — plus the e-invoicing layer Pagero, B2Brouter and Qvalia — added in this run.
  • Category note: Dime Scheduler is BC-native but in a different category (scheduling) — included as an ISV neighbour, not a direct AP rival.
  • LinkedIn engagement (likes/comments per post) is not included — requires a social listening tool.
  • YouTube figures per video for the last month cannot be reliably fetched automatically; only activity level can be described.
